What to Expect During Your Pet’s Annual Check-Up

RTM Std
February 26, 2025

An annual check-up is an essential part of maintaining your pet’s health and ensuring they lead a long, happy life. This routine visit allows the vet to monitor your pet's overall condition and catch any potential health concerns early. Here’s what you can expect during your pet's annual check-up:

  1. Physical Examination

    2. The vet will begin with a thorough physical exam, checking your pet’s ears, eyes, mouth, teeth, and coat. They'll assess their skin for signs of allergies, parasites, or infections, and examine their joints and muscles for signs of stiffness or discomfort. The vet will also listen to their heart and lungs using a stethoscope to detect any abnormalities.

  2. Vaccinations and Boosters

    3. Your pet’s vaccinations will be reviewed to ensure they’re up to date. Depending on your pet’s age, lifestyle, and risk factors, certain vaccines (like rabies, distemper, or kennel cough) may need to be updated or boosted. If your pet is due for any, the vet will administer the necessary shots.

  3. Parasite Prevention

    4. A big part of the check-up will include parasite prevention. Your vet will recommend flea, tick, and heartworm preventatives based on your pet's environment and lifestyle. If necessary, they'll test for parasites and discuss any treatments if needed.

  4. Weight and Body Condition

    5. Maintaining a healthy weight is crucial to your pet’s well-being. The vet will check your pet’s body condition and provide advice on their diet, portion sizes, and exercise routines to help keep them at a healthy weight. Obesity can lead to numerous health problems, so this step is key to ensuring your pet’s long-term health.

An annual check-up is a proactive way to ensure your pet stays healthy and happy. It’s a comprehensive visit that helps detect potential issues early, maintain proper care, and adjust any treatments as necessary. Regular check-ups can prevent serious health problems, extend your pet’s lifespan, and enhance the bond you share with them.
